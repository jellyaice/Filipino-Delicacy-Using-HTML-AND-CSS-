DISCOVER PHILIPPINES

The rainy season is upon us which means it's the perfect weather to cozy up with a bowl of heartwarming champorado. While it's a Filipino delicacy that's traditionally served with milk and some dried salted fish on the side, some have even taken it up a notch and added their own twist. Champorado is a sweet chocolate rice porridge that uses sweet glutinous rice (locally known as malagkit) and cocoa powder as main ingredients. A more traditional approach would be using tableya (pure cocoa blocks) instead of cocoa powder.
